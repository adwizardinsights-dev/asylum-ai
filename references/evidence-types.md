# Common Evidence Types in Asylum Cases

This is a general informational list. Not all items will apply to every case. Consult an attorney to determine what is relevant to your situation.

## Personal Documentation
- Personal declaration / written narrative (detailed account of events)
- Identity documents (passport, national ID, birth certificate)
- Entry documents (I-94, visa records, travel itinerary)

## Evidence of Harm or Threat
- Police reports (from home country or U.S.)
- Medical records documenting injuries or trauma
- Photographs of injuries, destroyed property, or threatening events
- Threatening letters, messages, or communications
- Death certificates of family members who suffered harm

## Country Condition Evidence
- U.S. State Department Country Reports on Human Rights
- UNHCR reports and guidance documents
- Reports from human rights organizations (Amnesty International, Human Rights Watch)
- News articles from reputable sources
- Academic research on country conditions

## Witness and Corroboration
- Affidavits or letters from witnesses
- Letters from community or religious leaders
- Expert witness declarations (country condition experts)

## Supporting Personal Documents
- Church, mosque, or temple membership records (for religious persecution)
- Political party membership records
- Employment records
- School records

## Notes
- Documents in languages other than English typically need certified translation
- Original documents are preferred when available
- Some evidence may not be obtainable — explain why in your declaration

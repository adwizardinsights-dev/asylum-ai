# Asylum Interview Preparation (General Information)

## Types of Interviews
- **Asylum Officer Interview** (affirmative process): Conducted by a USCIS asylum officer
- **Immigration Judge Hearing** (defensive process): More formal court setting

## General Interview Tips (Informational)
- Be consistent with your written application
- Answer the question asked — don't volunteer unrelated information
- If you don't understand a question, ask for clarification
- It is okay to say "I don't know" or "I don't remember exactly"
- Bring originals and copies of all supporting documents
- Bring a qualified interpreter if needed (some interviews provide one)

## Common Interview Topic Areas
- Your personal and family background
- Why you left your country
- What happened to you (specific incidents, dates, people involved)
- Whether you reported incidents to authorities in your home country
- Why you cannot return
- Your activities in the United States

## What to Bring
- All filed immigration documents (copy of I-589 and any supporting materials)
- Identity documents
- Supporting evidence
- Any attorney contact information

## After the Interview
- An asylum officer may grant asylum, refer to immigration court, or issue a Notice to Appear
- Timelines vary significantly

*Source: General public information. Not legal advice. Outcomes vary by individual case.*

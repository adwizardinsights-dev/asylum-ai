# Asylum Basics

Asylum is a form of protection that allows individuals who meet the definition of a refugee to remain in the United States.

## General Eligibility Criteria (Informational Only)
To be considered for asylum, a person generally needs to demonstrate:
- They have suffered persecution or have a well-founded fear of future persecution
- The persecution is based on one of five protected grounds
- The government of their country is either responsible for the persecution or unable/unwilling to control it

## The Five Protected Grounds
1. Race
2. Religion
3. Nationality
4. Political opinion
5. Membership in a particular social group (PSG)

## Filing Timeframe
Generally, individuals must apply within one year of arriving in the United States. There are exceptions for changed or extraordinary circumstances.

## Types of Asylum
- **Affirmative asylum**: Filed with USCIS when not in removal proceedings
- **Defensive asylum**: Raised as a defense in immigration court

## Key Documents Commonly Referenced
- Form I-589 (Application for Asylum and Withholding of Removal)
- Country condition reports (State Department, UNHCR)
- Personal declaration/affidavit
- Supporting evidence (medical records, police reports, photographs, witness letters)
